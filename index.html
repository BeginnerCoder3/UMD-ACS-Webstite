About the chapter

A community built around chemistry.

A DNA double helix, rendered live. Every major welcome.

The ACS Student Chapter at UMD is open to any undergraduate or graduate student curious about chemistry. We promote the field through scientific rigor, professional mentorship, and real community engagement.

We help members find career and research guidance, build a network on and off campus, and develop a lasting commitment to service — so you leave ready for whatever comes next in STEM.

Every major welcome

Events & programs.

Speaker nights, elementary-school outreach, and lab tours throughout the year. Add any of them to your calendar in one click.

Why join

Three good reasons.

Professional development

Resume workshops, mock interviews, and talks with chemists from industry and the faculty — real practice for the skills grad-school and job applications ask for.

Mentorship & networking

Get to know upperclassmen, alumni, and professors who've done what you're trying to do. It's how members find research advisors, study partners, and friends.

Outreach & service

We visit local schools, run demos, and take on service projects. You give back to the community — and earn your service hours while you're at it.

How to join.

Getting involved takes about three steps.

1

Register

Fill out the membership form to get on our roster and start receiving event updates.

2

Attend events

Come to at least three events a semester — a speaker night, community service, a lab tour, or a social.

3

Give back

Finish your service hours each semester through our outreach events and stay in good standing.

Eligibility

Membership requirements

  • Current UMD undergraduate or graduate student.
  • Taking, or having taken, at least one CHEM or BCHM course at UMD.
  • Attend three events per semester and complete the service hours.
  • Maintain at least a 2.0 GPA.
  • Genuine interest in STEM, networking, and community service.
  • National ACS membership is optional.
