About the Chapter

A community built around chemistry.

The ACS Student Chapter at the University of Maryland is open to chemistry and biochemistry students, and to anyone who finds the subject interesting. We want to help you connect what you learn in class with what happens outside of it.

We run professional development workshops, pair members with mentors, organize community outreach, and offer free peer tutoring. The goal is to give you the people, skills, and support to do well in your courses and after you graduate.

The Payoff

Why Join ACS?

Here is what you get out of being a member.

Professional Development

We run resume workshops, mock interviews, and speaker events with chemists from industry and from the faculty. It is good practice for the skills that come up on grad school and job applications.

Build your skills

Mentorship & Networking

Get to know upperclassmen, alumni, and professors who have done what you are trying to do. It is how a lot of our members find research advisors, study partners, and friends.

Find your people

Community Outreach & Service

We visit local schools, run demos, and take on service projects. You give back to the community and earn your service hours while you are at it.

Onboarding

How to Join

Getting involved takes about three steps.

1

Register

Fill out the membership form to get on our roster and start getting updates about events.

2

Show Up

Come to at least three events a semester. That can be a speaker event, outreach, a lab tour, or a social.

3

Give Back

Finish your service hours each semester through our outreach events and stay in good standing.

Membership Requirements

  • Membership is open to all current University of Maryland undergraduate and graduate students.
  • To be considered an active member, students must have taken or currently be taking at least one CHEM or BCHM course during their time at UMD, attend at least three events per semester, complete the required service hours each semester, and be in good standing with the University of Maryland to participate in extracurricular activities.
  • National American Chemical Society membership is optional.
